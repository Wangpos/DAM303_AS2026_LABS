2. Background
2.1 Why Distribution Over Return?
Standard DQN learns Q(s,a) = E[G|s,a] — a single number. But the actual return is random
(because the environment or the policy may be stochastic). C51 learns Z(s,a) — a full
probability distribution over G. The expected value of Z gives the same number as Q, but the
full distribution tells you much more: is the return reliably around 10, or is it bimodal
(sometimes -50, sometimes +50)?
